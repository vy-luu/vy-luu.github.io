GrafittiBot

Automatic Drawing Machine

GraffitiBot is a self-expressing spirograph machine, capable of drawing on whatever it is placed on. It is made out of laser cut plywood sides, connected by finger joints, and is controlled by an arduino-powered servo. The machine has a unique modular design where multiple layers are stacked on top of one another, making for customizability as well as simple servicing. The top layer houses a 9V battery that powers the arduino and servo, as well as two switches: one to turn power on and the other to power the servo on. The next layer houses the gears; the servo turns a flywheel, different sized gears, and a circular rack that connect to the drawing instruments. The next layer down houses the lights that brighten the drawing surface. In this layer, another 9V battery powers a series of green LEDs with the help of a 220Î© resistor. And finally, the next few layers gives the machine adjustable height to accommodate different sized pens and markers, as well as fasten the paper and machine to the ground.
